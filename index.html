I'm an ML Engineer at ALL3D where my responsibilities encompass a wide range of tasks, including designing, developing, and deploying machine learning models and systems, as well as optimizing their performance by identifying bottlenecks and implementing improvement strategies. I'm also involved with integration of ML systems, data analysis and preprocessing to inform model enhancements.

Before joining ALL3D, I was a Natural Language Understanding (NLU) Research Assistant at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. I worked under the guidance of Kaiser Pister to develop advanced solutions to bridge the gap between raw Large Language Model (LLM) performance and reliable software engineering, treating prompts as testable artifacts. This research culminated in the publication of PromptSet (ICSE '24) and the development of PromptDoctor (ESEM '25), tools designed to automatically identify bias and improve the reliability of LLM integrations in production environments.

I studied Computer Science and Data Science at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. My long-term professional aspirations encompass becoming a technical leader capable of translating research into robust, real-world systems.
